About LIB

LIB is a digital library catalog designed with a focus on clarity, accessibility, and ease of use. It helps users browse and search for books through a simple and structured interface that works well across devices and for users with different needs.

Project purpose

The goal of LIB is to make it easy for users to explore books and learning resources in a clear and accessible way. Instead of replicating a full commercial library system, the project focuses on clean design, simple navigation, and inclusive access to information.

The site is built to support readable content, organized structure, and straightforward interactions so users can quickly find what they need without confusion.

Target audience

LIB is designed primarily for students and researchers who need an efficient way to search and browse books. It also supports educators looking for reading materials and casual readers exploring topics by interest.

The site is intended for users with different levels of technical experience and is optimized for both desktop and mobile use. It also supports accessibility needs, including keyboard navigation and screen readers.

Design goals

The design focuses on clear structure, consistent components, and low cognitive load. Content is organized so users can scan and understand information quickly without unnecessary complexity.

Accessibility goals

The site follows accessibility best practices, including semantic HTML, keyboard-friendly navigation, readable text, and clear focus states. It is designed to work well with assistive technologies and provide an inclusive experience for all users.

Technical approach

The website is built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript with a focus on simplicity and performance. It includes a responsive layout, a searchable and filterable catalog, and accessible forms with helpful feedback.
